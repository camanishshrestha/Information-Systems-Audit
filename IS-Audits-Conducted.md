## 🎯 Project Objective

As financial institutions become increasingly dependent on technology, **Information Systems (IS) Audit** has emerged as one of the most critical assurance functions — evaluating whether IT systems are **secure, reliable, governed, and aligned with business objectives**. Across multiple engagements, I conducted IS Audits for commercial banks and subsequently led a comprehensive **IT GAP Assessment** against **ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A Controls** for a group of companies — combining my dual expertise as a **Chartered Accountant and Certified Information Systems Auditor (CISA®)**.

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step: What I Did

---

### ✅ Step 1: IS Audit Planning & Scope Definition

- Reviewed the institution's **IT infrastructure landscape** — including core banking systems, network architecture, data centers, and third-party integrations
- Studied relevant **regulatory requirements** applicable to the institution:
  - NRB Information Technology Guidelines for Banks and Financial Institutions
  - ISACA IS Audit and Assurance Standards
  - ISO/IEC 27001 Information Security Management requirements
- Defined the **IS audit scope** covering:
  - IT Governance & Strategy
  - Information Security Management
  - Access Control & Identity Management
  - Network & Infrastructure Security
  - Core Banking System Controls
  - Data Integrity & Backup Management
  - Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery (BCP/DRP)
  - Change Management & Patch Management
  - Vendor & Third-Party Risk Management
- Developed a **Risk-Based IS Audit Program** prioritizing high-risk IT domains
- Conducted **preliminary interviews** with IT management and system administrators to understand the control environment

---

### ✅ Step 2: IT Governance & Strategy Review

- Evaluated the institution's **IT Governance framework** against best practices (COBIT 2019)
- Reviewed:
  - **IT Strategy** — Alignment of IT objectives with overall business strategy
  - **IT Policies & Procedures** — Existence, completeness, and currency of IT policies
  - **IT Organizational Structure** — Roles, responsibilities, and reporting lines within the IT department
  - **IT Steering Committee** — Functioning, meeting frequency, agenda quality, and decision-making effectiveness
  - **IT Budget & Resource Management** — Adequacy of IT budget allocation and resource planning
  - **IT Risk Management** — Existence of IT risk register, risk assessment frequency, and risk treatment plans
- Assessed whether **IT decisions were governed at the Board/Senior Management level** with appropriate oversight
- Identified gaps in IT governance maturity and provided recommendations for improvement

---

### ✅ Step 3: Information Security Management Review

- Reviewed the institution's **Information Security Management System (ISMS)** for design and effectiveness
- Evaluated key information security controls including:
  - **Information Security Policy** — Existence, Board approval, communication to staff, and periodic review
  - **Asset Management** — IT asset inventory, classification, and ownership assignment
  - **Data Classification Policy** — Classification of data by sensitivity (Public, Internal, Confidential, Restricted)
  - **Encryption Standards** — Encryption of data at rest and in transit (SSL/TLS, AES standards)
  - **Email Security** — Anti-phishing, anti-spam, and secure email gateway controls
  - **Endpoint Security** — Antivirus/EDR deployment, patch status, and device management policies
  - **Physical Security** — Data center access controls, CCTV surveillance, visitor management, and environmental controls (fire suppression, UPS, cooling)
- Tested whether security controls were **operating effectively** — not just documented
- Identified security vulnerabilities and classified them by **risk severity** (Critical / High / Medium / Low)

---

### ✅ Step 4: Access Control & Identity Management Audit

> This was one of the most critical areas — **who has access to what, and whether that access is appropriate**

- Reviewed **Logical Access Controls** across core banking systems, network infrastructure, and applications:
  - **User Account Management** — Processes for user creation, modification, and deletion (joiners, movers, leavers)
  - **Privileged Access Management (PAM)** — Controls over system administrators, database administrators, and super users
  - **Password Policy** — Complexity, minimum length, expiry, and account lockout settings
  - **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)** — Deployment status across critical systems and remote access
  - **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)** — Appropriateness of access rights based on job roles
  - **Segregation of Duties (SoD)** — Identification of SoD conflicts in system access profiles
  - **Dormant/Inactive Accounts** — Detection and disabling of inactive user accounts
  - **Shared/Generic Accounts** — Identification and remediation of shared system accounts
- Extracted **user access listings** from core banking and network systems and performed:
  - Access entitlement reviews against current job roles
  - Detection of **excess privileges** and **unauthorized access**
  - Review of **terminated employee accounts** for timely revocation
- Documented all access control findings with supporting evidence

---

### ✅ Step 5: Network & Infrastructure Security Review

- Reviewed the institution's **network architecture** for security design adequacy
- Assessed:
  - **Network Segmentation** — Proper segmentation of internal network zones (DMZ, internal LAN, server zones, guest networks)
  - **Firewall Configuration** — Review of firewall rule sets for overly permissive or outdated rules
  - **Intrusion Detection/Prevention Systems (IDS/IPS)** — Deployment, alerting, and monitoring effectiveness
  - **VPN & Remote Access Security** — Controls over remote access channels, especially post-pandemic expansion
  - **Wireless Network Security** — WPA2/3 encryption, guest network isolation, rogue access point detection
  - **Network Monitoring & Logging** — SIEM/log management implementation and log retention policies
  - **Vulnerability Management** — Frequency of vulnerability scans, penetration testing, and patch remediation timelines
- Reviewed **network diagrams** for accuracy and completeness
- Identified **single points of failure** and **redundancy gaps** in network infrastructure

---

### ✅ Step 6: Core Banking System (CBS) Controls Review

- Reviewed application-level controls within **Core Banking Systems** (Finacle, Pumori, T24 — depending on institution)
- Assessed:
  - **Application Access Controls** — User roles, menu-level access restrictions, and maker-checker controls
  - **Transaction Authorization Controls** — Dual authorization for high-value transactions
  - **Audit Trail & Logging** — Completeness and integrity of CBS transaction logs
  - **System Parameter Controls** — Controls over changes to critical system parameters (interest rates, fee structures, limits)
  - **Interface Controls** — Data integrity controls at interfaces between CBS and other systems (SWIFT, payment gateways, regulatory reporting systems)
  - **Batch Processing Controls** — Completeness and accuracy of end-of-day batch processing
  - **Error Handling & Exception Reports** — Review and follow-up of system-generated exception reports
- Verified that **maker-checker (four-eyes principle)** was enforced for all critical transactions
- Identified instances where **system controls were overridden** without proper authorization

---

### ✅ Step 7: Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery (BCP/DRP) Review

- Evaluated the institution's **Business Continuity Plan (BCP)** and **Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP)** for adequacy
- Reviewed:
  - **Recovery Time Objective (RTO)** and **Recovery Point Objective (RPO)** definitions and achievability
  - **DR Site Infrastructure** — Adequacy of DR site setup, hardware capacity, and network connectivity
  - **Data Backup Management** — Backup frequency, backup types (full/incremental/differential), off-site backup storage, and backup restoration testing
  - **DR Drill / BCP Testing** — Frequency, scope, and outcomes of DR drills and BCP exercises
  - **Crisis Communication Plan** — Notification procedures for IT incidents and disasters
  - **Vendor Dependencies** — Critical vendor SLAs and their BCP arrangements
- Assessed whether **RTO/RPO commitments were realistically achievable** based on current DR infrastructure
- Identified gaps between documented BCP/DRP and actual recovery capabilities

---

### ✅ Step 8: Change Management & Patch Management Review

- Reviewed the institution's **IT Change Management process** for structured and controlled change implementation
- Assessed:
  - **Change Request Process** — Formal change request, impact assessment, approval, testing, and deployment workflow
  - **Change Advisory Board (CAB)** — Existence and functioning of CAB for change approval governance
  - **Emergency Change Procedures** — Controls over urgent/emergency changes to prevent unauthorized modifications
  - **Patch Management** — Patch identification, testing, approval, and deployment timelines for OS, applications, and firmware
  - **Software Version Control** — Version control practices for application code and configuration files
- Identified instances of **unauthorized changes, unpatched systems**, and **inadequate testing** before production deployment

---

### ✅ Step 9: IT GAP Assessment Against ISO/IEC 27001:2022 — Sanima Group (9 Companies)

> This was a **landmark engagement** — conducted as **Internal Audit Head** for Sanima Group of Companies

- Performed a **comprehensive IT GAP Assessment** measuring the current state of Information Security controls against the **ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A Controls** across 9 group companies

#### 🔹 Methodology:

- Mapped all **93 controls across 4 themes** of ISO 27001:2022 Annex A:
  - Organizational Controls (37 controls)
  - People Controls (8 controls)
  - Physical Controls (14 controls)
  - Technological Controls (34 controls)
- For each control, assessed:
  - **Current Implementation Status** (Implemented / Partially Implemented / Not Implemented)
  - **Maturity Level** (using a 1–5 maturity scale)
  - **Gap Description** — What is missing or deficient
  - **Risk Impact** — Potential consequences of the gap
  - **Recommended Action** — Specific remediation steps
- Conducted **structured interviews** with IT heads, department managers, and system users across group companies
- Performed **document reviews** of existing IT policies, procedures, system configurations, and security records
- Conducted **on-site observations** of physical security controls, server room conditions, and workstation security

#### 🔹 Key Assessment Areas Covered:

| ISO 27001:2022 Theme | Key Controls Assessed |
|---------------------|----------------------|
| **Organizational** | Information security policies, roles & responsibilities, threat intelligence, supplier relationships, incident management, business continuity |
| **People** | Screening, employment terms, security awareness training, disciplinary process, remote working |
| **Physical** | Physical perimeter security, secure areas, equipment security, clear desk/screen policy, supporting utilities |
| **Technological** | Access control, authentication, encryption, malware protection, logging, network security, SDLC security, vulnerability management, data masking, DLP |

- Prepared a **comprehensive IT GAP Assessment Report 2025** covering:
  - Executive Summary with overall maturity score
  - Control-by-control gap analysis
  - Heatmap of risk areas by severity
  - Prioritized remediation roadmap
  - Short-term, medium-term, and long-term recommendations
- Presented findings to **Senior Management and Board** of Sanima Group

---

### ✅ Step 10: IS Audit Report Preparation & Follow-Up

- Consolidated all IS audit findings into structured **IS Audit Reports** covering:
  - Executive Summary & Overall IT Risk Rating
  - Scope, Objectives & Methodology
  - Key Findings — classified by risk severity (Critical / High / Medium / Low)
  - Detailed finding descriptions with:
    - Observation
    - Risk/Impact
    - Root Cause
    - Recommendation
    - Management Response
  - Summary Dashboard — findings by domain and risk rating
- Communicated findings to **IT Management, Senior Management, and Audit Committee**
- Established **Issue Tracking Log** for monitoring remediation of IS audit findings
- Conducted **follow-up reviews** to verify implementation of agreed action plans
